# Value Proposition for Lead Partners: INNALAB-CURE Project

## Overview
INNALAB-CURE is Ukraine’s first mobile scientific and restoration laboratory. We are looking for an EU-based institution (ideally in Poland) to serve as a **Lead Partner** for an Interreg NEXT PL-UA grant and a co-applicant for Horizon Europe CL2.2.

---

## 1. Minimal Financial Risk, High Financial Impact
*   **90% EU Funding:** The project (Track A: Interreg NEXT PL-UA FMP) is 90% funded by the EU.
*   **In-Kind Contribution:** The Lead Partner’s required contribution (~€5,800) is strictly **in-kind** (expert hours, use of facilities). No direct financial injection is required.
*   **Funding Scale:** Immediate access to €99,510 for Track A, with a roadmap to Horizon Europe projects valued between €1M–€3M.

## 2. Unparalleled Scientific Data
*   **Unique Conflict-Zone Datasets:** Access to raw XRF, FTIR, and Raman spectroscopy data from heritage objects in active conflict zones. This data is non-existent in current EU datasets and provides a foundation for groundbreaking research in material degradation and emergency conservation.
*   **Provenance & Attribution:** Opportunity to co-author findings on newly attributed works (following our 2023 discovery of a Georges de La Tour painting).

## 3. Institutional Prestige and Visibility
*   **Lead Status:** Your institution will be the primary name on all EU documentation, reports, and public press releases.
*   **Policy Leadership:** Position your institution as a pioneer in the "Heritage First Responder" (HFR) framework—a new professional standard for cultural heritage protection during crises.
*   **UNESCO/ALIPH Context:** Collaboration on objects of national and world significance (UNESCO sites in Lviv, Kyiv, etc.).

## 4. Academic and Professional Growth
*   **Joint Publications:** Guaranteed co-authorship in high-impact journals such as *Heritage Science*, *Journal of Cultural Heritage*, and peer-reviewed EU publications.
*   **HFR Certification:** Authority to issue Heritage First Responder credentials, establishing your faculty as a regional hub for emergency restoration training.
*   **Curriculum Development:** Integrate real-world emergency conservation case studies from Ukraine into your university’s curriculum.

## 5. Proven Expertise & Infrastructure
*   **Ready-to-Deploy Team:** Our team has 4+ years of shared experience on UNESCO sites and 30+ years of professional practice (Lead: Inna Dmytruk-Sorokhtei).
*   **State-of-the-Art MobiBOX:** Fully autonomous mobile lab equipped with Bruker XRF, ThermoFisher FTIR, and digital X-ray—all compliant with IP67 safety standards.

---

## The Partnership Roadmap
1.  **Phase 1 (Interreg NEXT):** Establish the mobile unit, conduct 10-15 missions, create the HFR training module.
2.  **Phase 2 (Horizon Europe):** Expand the network to a Pan-European "Digital Library of Heritage at Risk" and scale the HFR program across the EU.
